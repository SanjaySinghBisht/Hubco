All About NPO/NGO/Section 8 Company Registration

What is a NPO - Section 8 Company?

Section 8 company or NPO or NGO are similiar in nature involves those activities which are done without the profit motive. Under Companies Act, NPO/NGO are registered under Section 8 and hence they are also called section 8 Company. NPO can raise funding by way of donation, corporate Social Responsibility (CSR) etc.

How to register NPO/NGO/Section 8 in India?

Registering a section 8 company or NGO or NPO involves various activities during registration process which has been broadly classified into three main heads:

#Apply for Name Approval:The first step after filing for DSC/DIN is to apply for name approval which could be like ABC Foundation etc. Once the name is approved, we need to proceed to next step for NGO/NPO/ section 8 company registration.

#File for CG Approval: After name is approvel, the next step is to file for central Government approval. Once CG approval is granted, one can easily register the section 8 Company. This is one of the most important step of section 8 company registration process.

#File for Incorporation: Once CG approval is granted, now we can proceed for section 8 company incorporation.

Why to register a NPO/NGO/Section 8 Com.?

If you are interested in some social welfare and planning to start a NPO/NGO or section 8 Company, then you should go for section 8 company registration. Further, you can also apply for section 80G and section 12AA registration for trust funding.

Requirements for NPO/NGO Registration

It require minimum of two persons to register for NPO/NGO or section 8 company in India. There is no need of any minimum paid up capital, further, funding under NGO/NPO get easier if the following two registrations are also applied:

  • - Section 12AA Registration to get exemption from Income tax Act, 1961.
  • - Section 80G registration to provide tax exemption to every person donates to your company. You can also apply for Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR).
